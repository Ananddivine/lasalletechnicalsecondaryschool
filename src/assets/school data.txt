Brief History of La Salle Technical Secondary School - Hohola
La Salle Technical Secondary School (LTSSH) is a Catholic Agency School under the PNG Department of Education, administered by the De La Salle Brothers and owned by the Catholic Archdiocese of Port Moresby.

The school began as Hohola Youth Development Centre (HYDC) in 1973, founded by the Brigidine Sisters from Australia to provide education and skills training for youth who had dropped out of traditional schooling.

Located at Allotment 01, Section 05, Oak Street, Hohola, Boroko, NCD, LTSSH has proudly served the community for 53 years.

History & Development
1973: HYDC established to teach employable skills and personal development.

1993: Administration transferred to De La Salle Brothers; student numbers grew to 350 by 1999.

2000: Expanded curriculum to include basic studies, vocational subjects, and work experience, addressing low secondary school enrolment rates for girls.

2000–2012: AusAID funding enabled construction of 14 classrooms, workshops for Auto-mechanics, Metal Fabrication, Carpentry, and later a Multi-purpose Hall, Administration Office, and Electro-Technology facilities.

2014–2015: Students ranked among the top 270 in FODE National Examinations.

2021: Transition to National Curriculum for Grades 9 and 10.

2022: Official registration as a Technical Secondary School under the Department of Education.

2024: LTSSH ranked 1st in the Grade 10 National Examinations.

2025: LTSSH ranked 1st in the Grade 10 & 12 National Examinations.

Child and Adults at Risk Safeguarding Commitment Statement  
The Lasallian District of Australia, New Zealand, Papua New Guinea, and Pakistan is committed to ensuring that children and adults at risk are safeguarded from harm in all Lasallian ministries.

Safeguarding is the responsibility of all. Although it acknowledges that children and people at risk may be vulnerable, it also recognizes an intrinsic value of human life. Every Lasallian Ministry must uphold and demonstrate a commitment to the safety and protection of children and adults at risk.

Lasallian Ministries must be places of safety and all personnel must uphold and demonstrate a commitment to the safety and protection of children and adults at risk. The tolerance and attention to child abuse and neglect across the District is expected and reinforced through the commitment to safeguarding.

Our commitment to safeguarding includes:

Robust recruitment practices (screening, psychological assessment, probationary period)

Mandatory induction

Ongoing safeguarding professional learning

Clear expectations of conduct

Commitment also includes information sharing, reporting, compliance with safeguarding policies, evolution of practices, monitoring standards, and vigilance with consideration for diverse backgrounds.

STUDENT HANDBOOK 2021 – The Six Patrons of Our School
Blessed Brother Arnold Reche, FSC  
Born 1838, Landroff, Lorraine, France. Wagon driver, later coachman. Known for piety, sobriety, wisdom. Entered Brothers at 22. Taught 14 years at Reims. Instructed soldiers in Franco-Prussian War, awarded Bronze Cross. Director of Nancy, later superior in Rheims. Known for devotion to Holy Spirit, gentleness, prayer, holiness. Died at 52.

St. Benildus Romançon, FSC  
Born in France, baptismal name Pierre Romançon. Frail but intelligent and industrious. Accepted into Brothers despite small stature. Strict but fair disciplinarian. School became center of village life. Devoted catechist, effective in first communion training. Died 1862 at 56. Beatified 1948, canonized 1967. Teaching was his profession, BROTHERING his ministry.

STUDENT HANDBOOK – Father Daniel Delaney & Saint Miguel Febres Cordero
Father Daniel Delaney  
Born 1747, Ireland. Penal Laws restricted Catholics. Studied abroad, ordained 1770. Curate of Tullow, Ireland. Founded Brigidine Sisters and Patrician Brothers. Concern for poor outstanding. Promoted education, music, and singing. Started choir, schools, and faith-sharing.

Saint Miguel Febres Cordero, FSC  
Born 1854, Cuenca, Ecuador. First native Ecuadorian Brother. Scholar, educator, nationally recognized. Published textbooks, translated books, wrote poetry. Taught in Spain later years. Canonized for commitment to education and faith. Died 1910.

Blessed Raphael Rafringa & Blessed Scubillion Rousseau
Blessed Raphael Rafringa, FSC  
Born Madagascar, 1856. Teacher, catechist. Joined Brothers 1881. Catechized and baptized over 3,000 Malagasy people. Director of Novitiate of St. Joseph (1904). Poet, hymn writer. Helped normalize France–Madagascar relations. Beatified June 29, 2009. Died 1919.

Blessed Scubillion Rousseau, FSC  
Born France, 1797. Joined Brothers at 25. Taught 34 years in France, then 34 years in Réunion Island. Catechized enslaved and freed people. Called “Apostle of the Slaves.” Beatified May 2, 1989. Died 1867.

Brother Scubilion – Apostle of the Slaves
Scubilion left France in 1833, dedicated 34 years to apostolate of slaves in Réunion. Opened evening classes, taught literacy, arithmetic, Christian dialogues. Helped slaves adapt to freedom. Blended catechism with history/current events. Afflicted with fever later years, cared for by slaves. Requested sacraments on last day. Venerated as saint.

Pope John Paul II titled him “Apostle of the Slaves” in 1989.
Born France, March 21, 1797. Died Réunion, April 13, 1867. Beatified May 2, 1989.
